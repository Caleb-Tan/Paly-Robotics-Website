Zero Robotics



Zero Robotics is an annual robot programming competition run by MIT that inspires students to test their engineering and leadership skills. Every year a unique game is released and teams of up to twenty students have one month to write a program that competes against opposing robots. Programs are fully autonomous and are designed to run on SPHERES (Synchronized Position Hold Engage and Reorient Experimental Satellites) which run on the International Space Station. This year's game is called SpySpheres and points are scored by facing the opposing SPHERE and taking pictures of them. Additional game elements include points packs which give bonuses to teams with fast movement, energy which limits game actions, and defensive mirrors that block incoming pictures.
